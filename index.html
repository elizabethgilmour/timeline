7.8 Magnitude Earthquake Hits Nepal

A 7.8 magnitude earthquake rocks Nepal, causing widespread damage across fourteen districts. Kathmandu Living Labs meets to brainstorm its earthquake response. That evening, Nama contacts Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team to seek help from the international OpenStreetMap community.

April 25

KLL Establishes Situation Room

Operating from a parking lot because its office was damaged in the earthquake, KLL establishes a situation room as the headquarters for its earthquake response.

KLL Deploys QuakeMap.org

KLL deploys QuakeMap and begins to work with the international community. On the same day, a powerful aftershock of magnitude 6.9 hits Nepal.

Read more April 26

Unprecedented Response from Volunteer Mappers

Within 48 hours, over 1,500 people begin to remotely map the affected areas using aerial imagery. Emphasizing the importance of maps, the army asks KLL for maps of the affected districts.

KLL produces the first situation report

KLL produces its first report from the situation room devoted to managing its earthquake response. The situation room report explains KLL’s actions in the relief effort and how others can help.

Read more April 27

Demand for Maps and Data Continues to Grow

Organizations begin to request printable maps for field work from KLL. Meanwhile, 2,200 volunteers have contributed through remote mapping.

April 28

KLL Guides Doctors’ Efforts

KLL holds a training session for doctors assisting with earthquake relief efforts. KLL members teach doctors how to use OSM and QuakeMap to determine where their help is needed. At this point, 3,300 mappers have assisted in OSM mapping.

April 30

KLL Launches QuakeRelief.net

To meet the need for printable maps of the affected districts, KLL introduces QuakeRelief, a repository of printable maps that use the data mapping volunteers have added to the OSM map of Nepal.

New York Times Covers KLL’s Work

The New York Times reports on KLL's earthquake response efforts, spreading the word about what KLL was doing and allowing KLL to accomplish more.

Read more May 1

Supporting Cultural Heritage Recovery

KLL begins working with UNESCO and the Department of Archeology to document the condition of cultural heritage sites in the Kathmandu Valley. KLL holds a training to show volunteers how to report the conditions of sites in an app.

May 2

Wired Magazine Explains the Role of Crisis Mapping

Wired Magazine covers the role of crisis mapping for the Nepal earthquake recovery and the importance of volunteer mapping efforts after disasters.

Read more May 8

Another Major Quake Hits Nepal

An aftershock of 7.1 magnitude further damages the affected districts and is a strong reminder of the importance of both recovering from disasters and preparing for future natural disasters.  This second earthquake makes KLL’s office too dangerous to enter.

Read more May 12

KLL Moves to a Temporary Office

KLL moves into a building at Kasthamandap School so it can continue to work. Schools have not yet reopened since the earthquake, so a school building can provide a temporary office.

May 16

Mapping Road Closures

KLL partners with the International Labor Organization to begin mapping road closures in OpenStreetMap.  On-the-ground mapping lets relief organizations know which locations can be reached by road, and where only pack animals and helicopters can go.

May 17

QuakeMap Reports Continue to Arrive

QuakeMap continues to be a vital tool in the earthquake response with 1,500 reports about earthquake damage and relief efforts. 

May 18

Earthquake-caused Landslide Blocks River

A landslide blocks the Kali Gandaki river, showing the need for continued mapping. It creates a temporary dam which leads to massive flooding upstream of the dam. 

Read more May 24

Landslide Hazard Mapping

KLL works with a geologist from Tribhuvan University to map landslide risks in Dhading district. The goal is to produce a system for predicting which homes are at risk of landslides and what measures locals can take to stay safe.

Read more June 10

New Office for KLL

Kathmandu Living Labs moves to a new office in Chundevi, Kathmandu. 

July 7

Crisis Informatics Workshop

KLL and the Harvard Humanitarian Initiative jointly hold a workshop to discuss the role of crisis informatics in the relief efforts following the April earthquake. 

Read more August 9

Google Interns Assist KLL

Google sends a team of employees to Kathmandu to assist in disaster recovery efforts. Two of them work at KLL and assist in KLL’s work. 

August 10

Preparing OSM for Future Disasters

Two members of KLL attend a workshop in Jakarta to learn about Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team activations and discuss how to better prepare for future disaster response.

September 18

UAV Mapping Workshop

KLL members attend a workshop on using Unmanned Aerial Vehicles, or drones, for humanitarian work. With new skills and new UAVs, KLL can expand its efforts in using UAVs for mapping.

September 23

October 25

The Reconstruction Phase Begins as the Earthquake Response Comes to a Close

Kathmandu Living Labs closes QuakeMap.org, marking the conclusion of the earthquake response effort. The Nepal government asks KLL to provide support for a housing damage assessment through data collection, and KLL also helps the Department of Education with a school infrastructure damage assessment.
