• UAV Mapping September 23, 2015
    KLL members attend a workshop on using Unmanned Aerial Vehicles, or drones, for humanitarian work. A UAV company donates four new UAVs to KLL so KLL can expand its efforts in using UAVs for mapping.
  • Google Helps August 10, 2015
    Google sends a team of employees to Kathmandu to assist in disaster recovery efforts. Two of them work at KLL and assist in KLL’s work.
  • Crisis Informatics August 9, 2015
    KLL and the Harvard Humanitarian Initiative jointly hold a workshop to discuss the role of crisis informatics in the relief efforts following the April earthquake.
  • Reporting Ends July 13, 2015
    QuakeMap enters a new phase in which volunteers continue to follow up on reports but QuakeMap no longer accepts new reports.
  • New Office July 7, 2015
    Kathmandu Living Labs moves to a new office in Chundevi, Kathamandu.
  • Landslide Mapping June 10, 2015
    KLL works with a geologist from Tribhuvan University to map landslide risks in Dhading district. The goal is to produce a system for predicting which homes are at risk of landslides.
  • River Blockage May 24, 2015
    A landslide blocks the Kali Gandaki river, showing the need for continued mapping. It creates an a temporary dam and which produces massive flooding upstream of the dam.
  • QuakeMap Continues May 18, 2015
    QuakeMap continues to be a vital tool in the earthquake response with 1,500 reports about earthquake damage and relief efforts.
  • Road Assessment May 17, 2015
    KLL partners with the International Labor Organization to begin mapping road closures in OpenStreetMap.
  • Temporary Office May 16, 2015
    KLL moves to Kasthamandap School and uses a school building as a temporary office.
  • 7.1 M Aftershock May 12, 2015
    Wired Magazine covers Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team’s efforts to map Nepal after the earthquake.
  • Wired Magazine May 8, 2015
    Wired Magazine covers Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team’s efforts to map Nepal after the earthquake.
  • BBC Reports May 6, 2015
    BBC reports on KLL’s mapping and how QuakeMap and open mapping were vital to the relief effort.
  • Printed Maps May 5, 2015
    The Canadian Department of Defence, which had been working on relief efforts in Nepal, produced printed maps from the OpenStreetMap data provided by volunteers.
  • NEA Collaboration May 3, 2015
    KLL begins working with the National Engineers Association for a damage assessment.
  • UNESCO Training May 2, 2015
    KLL holds its first training with UNESCO volunteers to document the condition of cultural heritage sites in the Kathmandu Valley. Volunteers learned to document the condition of sites and then georeference them.
  • QuakeRelief Introduced May 1, 2015
    To meet the need for printable maps of the affected districts, KLL introduces QuakeRelief, a repository of printable maps that use the data mapping volunteers have added to the OSM map of Nepal. The New York Times reports on KLL's earthquake response efforts.
  • Assisting Doctors April 30, 2015
    KLL holds a training for doctors assisting with the relief efforts. KLL members teach doctors how to use OSM and QuakeMap to determine where to go.
  • QuakeMap Reports April 29, 2015
    By this time, QuakeMap has recieved 445 reports or requests for aid.
  • Making Maps April 28, 2015
    Organizations begin to request printable maps for field work from KLL. Meanwhile, 2,200 people have contributed through remote mapping, and other organizations begin to request printable maps.
  • Mapping Begins April 27, 2015
    1,500 people remotely map the affected areas using aerial imagery. Emphasizing the need for maps, the army asks KLL for maps of the affected districts.
  • QuakeMap Deploys April 26, 2015
    Operating from a parking lot because its office was destroyed in the earthquake, KLL deploys QuakeMap. In addition, an aftershock of 6.9 magnitude is felt.
  • 7.8 M Earthquake April 25, 2015
    A 7.8 magnitude earthquake rocks Nepal, creating widespread damage across fourteen districts. Kathmandu Living Labs meets to plan its earthquake response.
